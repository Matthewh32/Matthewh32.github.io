Research Interests

My research interests include observational astronomy and theoretical astrophysics, with a focus on the properties of the most extreme objects in the universe, like the magnetic fields of black holes and neutron stars.


Research Experience

My research experience includes work at Brookhaven National Laboratory, where I contributed to a project integrating machine learning with image segmentation and analysis.

The goal of this project was to implement a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to identify and label the locations of oxidized copper atoms within Environmental Transmission Electron Microscopy (ETEM) data, with the hope of increasing the rate of image analysis without losing accuracy.

Using Python and its libraries, particularly TensorFlow and Keras, I trained the model on a dataset of ETEM images expanded with data augmentations like rotations and Gaussian blurs. This project has applications in image analysis workflows and could increase the rate of scientific breakthroughs related to the study of metal oxidation, and a research paper based on my contributions is currently under review at a major journal.


Observational Astronomy and Analysis Project

In collaboration with Biola University and one of its adjunct faculty members, I have been involved in collecting and analyzing observational data with Biola's Asto-Physics StarFire 160 EDF refractor telescope and Mach2 GTO mount. I am developing a Python-based image processing pipeline that calibrates, aligns, stacks, and background subtracts astronomical images while automatically generating postage stamps and an annotated final image for scientific analysis. This is an ongoing project with the goals of expanding Biola's astronomy capabilities and pushing the limits of the scope to produce publishable results.
